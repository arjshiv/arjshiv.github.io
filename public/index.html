01 About

About

My career has followed a consistent pattern: find a large, operationally messy system, build software that reveals the hidden signal inside it, and push that system toward better decisions. That pattern started in physics at Cornell, where I learned that I preferred building tools for other people to use over staying in the lab.

Narrative Thread

BlackRock proved that complex financial infrastructure could be made legible. Climb Credit proved that better models could open access. ResiDesk applies the same discipline to housing, where resident conversations become business intelligence.

At BlackRock, I worked on products that translated institutional-grade analytics into tools used by financial advisors and retirees. At Climb Credit, I led product, engineering, and data work that tied financing decisions to actual earnings outcomes. At ResiDesk, the focus shifted to everyday housing operations, where AI can turn high-volume conversations into measurable retention, revenue, and service insight.

The throughline is not novelty for novelty’s sake. It is applied AI in places where the stakes are practical, the market is large, and the results can be observed in adoption, operating leverage, and public recognition from the industries being changed.

ResiDesk

Co-Founder

ResiDesk focuses on a stubborn blind spot in housing: operators hear from residents constantly, but rarely in a way that compounds into decision-quality data. We built a system that learns from millions of resident interactions and turns them into action for leasing, retention, and operations. The consequence is not abstract. It shows up in higher renewals, stronger rent performance, and a better read on what residents actually value.

Climb Credit

CTO/CPO

Climb Credit was built around a simple challenge: financing education should depend on whether the education creates economic mobility. I led engineering, product, and data work around underwriting models that emphasized outcomes rather than extraction. That decision aligned growth with student success, helped scale annual loans from $1 million to $300 million, and supported students who saw materially better earnings outcomes.

BlackRock

Product & Engineering Lead

At BlackRock, the work centered on taking institutional analytics and making them usable for financial advisors at scale. My team helped build Aladdin for Retail so the core value was not hidden in quantitative complexity. By translating sophisticated risk tooling into something practical and legible, the product grew from zero to $40 million in annual recurring revenue in its first year.

07 Operating Principles

Operating Principles

These are not abstract preferences. They are the heuristics that have shaped which products I build, which companies I back, and which markets I think are worth the effort.

Adoption Compounds Unevenly

The biggest opportunities are in technologies that are already working but haven't been widely adopted yet. By the time something is obvious, the biggest gains have already happened. I look for ideas that seem weird to most people but obvious to experts in the field.

Unloved Problems Create Asymmetry

The best problems to solve aren't the ones everyone's working on. They're the ones that seem tedious, complex, or unglamorous. Real estate, education financing, and risk analysis all fit this description. These are massive markets where even small improvements create enormous value.

Human Judgment Should Sit Above the Model

The best AI systems aren't fully autonomous. They're tools that make humans more effective. This is why I focus on AI+human hybrid solutions. The goal isn't to replace people, but to let them work at a higher level of abstraction, handling the cases that require judgment while automating the rest.
