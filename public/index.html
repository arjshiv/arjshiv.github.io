01 What I do

What I do

I like markets with bad feedback loops. People are surrounded by information and still miss the signal that matters. That thread runs from physics at Cornell to BlackRock, Climb Credit, and ResiDesk.

The pattern

Find the buried signal. Build the tool people will use. Make it change a real decision.

At BlackRock, that meant making institutional risk tools usable for advisors. At Climb Credit, it meant underwriting around earnings outcomes. At ResiDesk, it means learning from resident conversations at scale and turning them into operating signal for owners and operators.

I don’t care about AI theater. I care about whether the loop closes faster and whether the result shows up in renewals, revenue, or speed.

ResiDesk

Co-Founder

Housing does not have a survey problem. It has a signal problem. Operators hear from residents all day and still miss what will lift renewals, hurt NOI, or push someone to leave. At ResiDesk, we use millions of resident interactions to make that visible. The work shows up in renewals, rent, and a clearer view of what residents stay and pay for.

Climb Credit

CTO/CPO

Climb started with a simple question: does this program change earnings? I led product, engineering, and data around underwriting that cared about that answer. The result helped grow annual loan volume from $1 million to $300 million.

BlackRock

Product & Engineering Lead

At BlackRock, the work was straightforward to describe and hard to do. Take institutional risk tools and make them usable for advisors. My team helped build Aladdin for Retail, and it reached $40 million in ARR in its first year.

07 How I reason

How I reason

A few rules keep showing up.

Adoption is lumpy

The best openings show up after something starts working and before consensus forms. By the time everyone agrees, the edge is gone.

Boring problems pay

Good markets often look dull from the outside. That is usually a gift. Housing, education finance, and risk tools all looked messy, slow, or unglamorous right before they got interesting.

Keep humans above the model

Models should make people faster and sharper. They should not take judgment off the table. The hard part is still the human part.
